Honkai: Star Rail (Chinese: 崩坏：星穹铁道; pinyin: Bēnghuài: Xīngqióng Tiědào; lit. 'Honkai: Star Dome Railway') is a role-playing gacha video game developed by miHoYo, published by miHoYo in China and worldwide by its subsidiary HoYoverse. It was released on April 26, 2023, for Windows and mobile devices; port for PlayStation 5 is planned with a Q4 release window, while the PlayStation 4 version is still yet to be announced, as revealed at the latest Summer Game Fest with a trailer. It is the fourth installment in the Honkai series, drawing on characters from Honkai Impact 3rd and gameplay elements from Genshin Impact.




Development

Honkai: Star Rail was revealed in October 2021 at the end of the Honkai Impact 3rd online concert, Starfire Sonorant, then later on the game's official YouTube channel.

The game entered its first closed beta testing later that month. A second closed beta test was held between May and June 2022. A final closed beta test was held in February 2023 and prior to the release of the game. In April 2023, HoYoverse announced having 10 million pre-registered users.




Gameplay

Honkai: Star Rail follows the gameplay style of classic Japanese role-playing games: players build up a lineup of characters, and control a team of four in turn-based combat.Elements of open-world and dungeon exploration are present, with multiple mechanics, including the gacha system, carried over from HoYoverse's previous action RPG Genshin Impact.

Story

The game is set in a science-fantasy universe in which humanity follows the paths of beings called Aeons. The player character, the Trailblazer, whose name and gender are chosen by the player, awakens without their memory aboard a space station and discovers that they are imbued with a Stellaron, a disruptive cosmic entity. They join a band of adventurers, the Trailblazers, traveling from world to world aboard the Astral Express, their space train.

The Trailblazer wakes up and meets two members of the crew, March 7th and Dan Heng. Both are traveling on the Astral Express, a large cosmic train that travels the universe along the eponymous "Star Rail". After eliminating the remaining threats, the Trailblazer meets Himeko, the Astral Express's engineer, and Welt, an interdimensional traveler from the Honkai Impact 3rd universe. In an attempt to discover their origins and eliminate the threats of the remaining Stellarons, the Trailblazer departs with the Astral Express crew.


Aeons

Aeon

The Aeons are godlike beings in Honkai: Star Rail who preside over the galaxy. Each Aeon is associated with a Path, by which they can use its energy as they please, but they are also bound to it for the rest of their lives.

People know very little about these mysterious existences that stroll through the vast stellar sea. Limited by inherent capacities of cognition, intelligent lifeforms can only faintly discern that Aeons tread upon Paths imperceptible to mortal beings and execute unfathomable powers via some form of belief. In legends passed down through word of mouth, the Aeons became the embodiment of highly condensed philosophical concepts in the end.

If anyone can tread on a Path ruled by an Aeon, they will be inundated by a distant sensation, like being struck by a glance from light-years away across the galaxy. Many believe that this is the only connection that Aeons and mortals can ever have with each other.

Path

"The birth of an Aeon gives rise to a Path. The nature of the Paths remains a mystery, leaving us to draw an analogy in a way that mortals can understand: It's a philosophical concept of sorts."

The Aeons can use Paths as they please after their ascension and are linked to it for the rest of their existence.

There are currently 18 known Paths, each with a corresponding Aeon and their own followers.

The Destruction

Those who follow the "Destruction" Path admire recklessness, anger, and destructive behavior.



The Hunt

Those who follow the "Hunt" Path admire determination, ruthlessness, and tenacious behavior.



The Erudition

Those who follow the "Erudition" Path admire thinking, logic, and strategic behavior.



The Harmony

Those who follow the "Harmony" Path admire understanding, support, and cooperative behavior.



The Nihility

Those who follow the "Nihility" Path admire laziness, exhaustion, and meaningless behavior.



The Preservation

Those who follow the "Preservation" Path admire patience, sacrifice, and defensive behavior.



The Abundance

Those who follow the "Abundance" Path admire selflessness, altruism, and healing behavior.

Stellaron

Stellaron

A Stellaron is a mysterious object spreading across the universe like a cancerous growth, leading it to be nicknamed the Cancer of All Worlds by the Interastral Peace Corporation. Stellarons are considered seeds of disaster which cause massive changes to civilizations and ecosystems, and result in the appearance of Fragmentum.

Various factions had started to notice a phenomenon since a specific time. A vague, imperceptible matter was disrupting the flow of Imaginary Energy along the interstellar routes created by the Trailblaze. Like a mountain climber meeting a cliff or a long-distance ship encountering tsunamis and whirlpools, interstellar travel changed from being unobstructed to being dangerous and unpredictable.

The Stellaron Hunters, who serve an enigmatic individual known as Elio, scour the world for Stellarons and have obtained several. Their intentions are currently unclear; Kafka appears to know the Trailblazer's background and implanted a Stellaron in their body for an unknown reason.

It is speculated that the Antimatter Legion and Nanook the Destruction may be involved with the appearance of Stellarons. This is due to the frequent appearance of Stellarons shortly after these destroyers of universes appear on worlds. It is currently unknown if these occurrences are related or just coincidence.

This phenomenon is remarkably aggressive. It continues to encroach upon neighboring worlds in the form of a mysterious object, the Stellaron. It keeps spreading all over the universe like a cancerous growth, leading the Interastral Peace Corporation to call it "Cancer of All Worlds" and warning interstellar travelers not to take it lightly. The worlds swamped by the Cancer of All Worlds were soon beyond saving, and very few people survived.

There are many speculations regarding the Cancer of All Worlds, and most are related to Nanook the Destruction and the Antimatter Legion. These destroyers of universes often seemed to appear together with Stellarons. We do not yet know if these occurrences are related or are only coincidences.

Nobody has found a way to destroy a Stellaron. Currently, the most that can be done is contain its energy.

Echoes of War

Echoes of War

Echoes of War are challenges that will open after completing specific Trailblaze or Companion Missions. During these challenges, the player will relive the experience of fighting Bosses encountered in the story and receive rewards such as Trace Materials, rare Light Cones, and Relics.

Players can complete Echoes of War three times a week, and the same Echo of War can be challenged multiple times. Trailblaze Power Trailblaze Power x30 is required to start an Echo of War and obtain its rewards. Reward availability is refreshed upon Weekly Reset.

Rules:
  • War memories of past formidable foes are still fresh. Use Echo of War to relive past battles and gain benefits. Consume Trailblaze Power to challenge and obtain rewards.
  • Advanced Trace Materials can be used to activate or upgrade Traces.
  • Trailblaze Power will constantly regenerate at a rate of 1 point every 6 minutes. You may exchange Stellar Jade for Trailblaze Power.
  • When Trailblaze Power has been fully regenerated or exceeds the maximum accumulation amount, it will no longer regenerate.

    • Refresh Rules:
  • Echo of War's weekly reward claim count will refresh every Monday at 04:00 AM (Server Time).
  • You can claim rewards up to 3 times across different Echo of War instances. Remember to get your rewards based on your needs.

    Forgotten Hall

    Forgotten Hall

    The Forgotten Hall involves a number of stages with increasing difficulties. Defeating all enemies in each stage of the Forgotten Hall will reward the player with EXP materials, Credits and Lucent Afterglow. In addition, extra rewards can be obtained by fulfilling specific bonus conditions. Equilibrium Levels will not affect the difficulty and rewards in Forgotten Hall stages.

    Forgotten Hall contains two types of stages, "Memory" and "Memory of Chaos:"

  • The former has 2 subtypes, "The Last Vestiges of Towering Citadel" containing 15 stages and "The Voyage of Navis Astriger" containing 6 stages.
  • The latter unlocks once all "Memory: The Last Vestiges of Towering Citadel" stages have been completed, featuring stages of higher difficulty. Its subtype, Memory Turbulence and its effects, being updated periodically, bringing new content and rewards.
  • All stages must be completed in order.


    • While in the Forgotten Hall, with the following actions temporarily disabled:
  • Changing team setup
  • Leveling up or ascending characters
  • Changing, leveling up, or ascending Light Cones and Relics
  • Using Consumables

    • All battles taking place in the Forgotten Hall must be won within a certain number of cycles. When the player runs out of cycles, the challenge will fail. The remaining cycles will be displayed on the action bar on the left-hand side.

    Simulated Universe

    Simulated Universe

    The gameplay revolves around collecting upgrades, known as Blessings, that have good synergy with each other and the team.

    It is the source for Herta Bond, which can used to buy items from Herta's Store.

    Gameplay Notes
    • Blessings are only active during the current run. After a defeat, the world has to be started from the beginning.
    • The Ability Tree unlocks permanent upgrades.
    • There are 7 worlds that have to be beaten in order.
    • There are 6 domain types.
    • Combat
      - contains a series of battles, with each battle granting a Blessing
    • Elite
      - contains a single more difficult Battle that grants a high rarity Blessing
    • Boss
      - contains the boss enemy
    • Occurence
      - contains a random event
    • Transaction
      - contains a point that allows players to buy or swap Blessings
    • Respite
      - contains all of the following
  • Breakable objects that give HP and Technique Points
  • Downloader that allows choosing an additional available teammate
  • Herta Puppet that gives a random event or the chance to upgrade Blessings
  • After unlocking in the Ability Tree, a device that allows reviving your characters

    • Simulated Universe Point Rewards

    Points are collected based on a player's progress throughout the worlds in the Simulated Universe. After accumulating a set amount of points, a player will receive a variety of rewards such as Credit, Stellar Jade, Lost Gold Fragments, Herta Bond, Star Rail Pass, and Tracks of Destiny. When a player's Equilibrium Level increases, a player's points tier will also increase. This increases the amount of rewards a player will receive.

    Rewards are reset once every week on Monday at 4:00 (Server Time).

    Herta Space Station is a space station founded by Herta, member #83 of the Genius Society. Researchers from different planets comes to work here as a researcher and most of them are faithful followers of Herta.
